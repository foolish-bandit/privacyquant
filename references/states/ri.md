# Rhode Island — RIDTPPA

**Citation form**: Rhode Island Data Transparency and Privacy Protection Act, R.I. Gen. Laws §§ 6-48.1-1 to -10. Effective Jan 1, 2026. Enforced by the Rhode Island Attorney General.

## Applicability

Persons conducting business in Rhode Island or producing products/services targeted to RI residents AND during a calendar year:

1. Control/process PD of **at least 35,000** RI consumers (excluding solely-payment-transaction data); OR
2. Control/process PD of **at least 10,000** RI consumers AND derive over **20%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

> Lowest-tier thresholds match DE, MD, NH.

Exemptions: standard.

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling. **Right to appeal** within 60 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

Required effective at law's effective date (Jan 1, 2026).

## Sensitive data

Standard categories. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments for high-risk activities.

## Distinctive RIDTPPA features

- **Specific transparency disclosures**: the privacy notice must identify, by name, all third parties with which the controller may share PD (more granular than categorical disclosure).
- **Heightened minor protections** consistent with the trend among newer state laws.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalties under RI consumer protection framework. Cure period at enactment.

## RI-specific drafting considerations

1. **Named third-party disclosure** in privacy notices is a significant transparency upgrade — most states accept categorical disclosure. Maintain an up-to-date list of third-party recipients tied to the privacy notice.
2. UOOM mandatory at effective date.
