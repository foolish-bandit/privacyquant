# Montana — MCDPA

**Citation form**: Montana Consumer Data Privacy Act, Mont. Code §§ 30-14-2801 to -2826. Effective Oct 1, 2024. Enforced by the Montana Attorney General.

## Applicability

Mont. Code § 30-14-2802. Applies to persons conducting business in Montana or producing products/services targeted to MT residents AND:

1. Controls/processes PD of **at least 50,000** MT consumers (excluding solely-payment-transaction data); OR
2. Controls/processes PD of **at least 25,000** MT consumers AND derives over **25%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

> **Lowest "primary tier" consumer threshold (50k)** of any state. Reflects MT's smaller population — proportionally captures the same cohort as 100k thresholds elsewhere.

Exemptions: government; non-profits; higher ed; HIPAA covered entities/BAs (entity-level); GLBA-covered financial institutions (entity-level); certain insurers; employment data; B2B.

## Consumer rights

Mont. Code § 30-14-2812. Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling. **Right to appeal** within 60 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

Required effective Jan 1, 2025.

## Sensitive data

Standard categories. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments for high-risk activities.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation**. **Cure period sunset April 1, 2026.**

## MT-specific drafting considerations

1. The 50k threshold catches mid-size businesses that are out of scope under the 100k tier in most states. Run the test independently — don't assume MT applicability tracks CO/CT.
2. UOOM recognition mandatory effective Jan 2025.
