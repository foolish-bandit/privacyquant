# New Jersey — NJDPA

**Citation form**: New Jersey Data Privacy Act, N.J. Stat. §§ 56:8-166.4 to -166.16. Effective Jan 15, 2025 (with an 18-month grace period before AG enforcement actions, except for willful violations). Enforced by the New Jersey Attorney General (Division of Consumer Affairs).

## Applicability

N.J. Stat. § 56:8-166.5. Applies to controllers conducting business in New Jersey or producing products/services targeted to NJ residents AND during a calendar year:

1. Control/process PD of **at least 100,000** NJ consumers (excluding solely-payment-transaction data); OR
2. Control/process PD of **at least 25,000** NJ consumers AND **derive revenue or receive a discount** on the price of goods/services from the sale of PD.

> The second tier is activity-based (any revenue from sale at 25k threshold) — NOT a percentage threshold. NJ is unusual in that any sale-derived revenue with 25k consumers triggers applicability.

Exemptions: financial institutions (entity-level GLBA); HIPAA (entity); FCRA; insurance; secured creditors; government; employment data; B2B.

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling with significant effects. **Right to appeal** within 45 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

Required effective July 15, 2025 (six months after effective date).

## Sensitive data

Defined at § 56:8-166.4. Includes standard categories plus **financial information** (account number, credit/debit card number, in combination with security/access code/password) and **status as transgender or non-binary**. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments.

## Minor protections

NJDPA imposes opt-in consent for processing of PD of consumers known to be **at least 13 and less than 17 years old** for purposes of targeted advertising, sale, or profiling for legal/significant effects.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$10,000 for first violation, $20,000 for subsequent violations** under the New Jersey Consumer Fraud Act. **18-month right to cure for non-willful violations** until July 2026; thereafter, AG discretion.

## NJ-specific drafting considerations

1. The **activity-based second-tier threshold** (any sale revenue + 25k consumers) is broader than most states' percentage thresholds. NJ catches mid-sized businesses that escape VA/CT.
2. **Financial information in sensitive data** is distinctive — most states cover this through other means but NJ's express inclusion broadens consent triggers.
3. **Minor protections to age 17** (one year higher than CT) are the broadest after MD.
4. UOOM required Jul 2025.
5. Higher per-violation penalties under NJ CFA framework.
