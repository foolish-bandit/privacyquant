# Florida — FDBR

**Citation form**: Florida Digital Bill of Rights, Fla. Stat. §§ 501.701 to 501.722. Effective July 1, 2024. Enforced by the Florida Department of Legal Affairs (Attorney General).

> **The narrowest applicability of any state privacy law.** FDBR was drafted to target a small set of large platform-scale entities, not as a general business-applicability statute.

## Applicability

Fla. Stat. § 501.702. Applies only to a "controller" that:

1. Is a **for-profit entity** that conducts business in Florida; AND
2. **Processes or engages in the sale of PD**; AND
3. **Makes ≥ $1 billion in global gross annual revenue**; AND
4. Satisfies ONE of:
   - Derives **≥50% of global gross annual revenue from the sale of advertisements online**;
   - Operates a **consumer smart-speaker / voice-command service** with cloud assistant integrated and connected to a cloud computing service; OR
   - Operates an **app store / digital distribution platform** offering ≥250,000 different software applications for consumers to download and install.

> Three of the four prongs are activity-targeted. Only large ad-revenue platforms (e.g., Meta, Google), large smart-speaker platforms (e.g., Amazon, Google), and major app stores (e.g., Apple, Google Play) typically satisfy any prong. Most businesses have NO obligations under FDBR even if their threshold-state analysis would otherwise place them in scope.

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling with significant effects. Right to appeal within 60 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

Required.

## Sensitive data

Standard categories with one notable addition: **biometric data, geolocation, and personal data revealing health diagnosis, racial or ethnic origin, religious beliefs, or sexual orientation** are explicitly enumerated.

**Opt-in consent required** to process.

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments.

**Specific notice requirements** for consumer-facing biometric and genetic data sales — a "DO NOT SELL MY [SENSITIVE PERSONAL/BIOMETRIC/GENETIC] DATA" disclosure structure.

## Children's protections

Florida has companion legislation (HB 3) imposing significant additional obligations regarding minors' use of online platforms, including age-verification and account restrictions for under-14s. Coordinate FDBR analysis with HB 3 if minor users are involved.

## Enforcement

Florida Department of Legal Affairs. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$50,000 per violation, tripled** for violations involving processing of a known minor's data, deletion of data without consent, or use of dark patterns to obtain consent. **45-day cure period.**

## FL-specific drafting considerations

1. **Most businesses are NOT subject to FDBR.** Run the threshold test specifically — do not assume Florida coverage just because the entity has Florida customers.
2. **Penalties up to $50,000/violation, trebled to $150,000** for minor-related violations are the highest in any state by a significant margin.
3. Companion legislation (HB 3 for minors, the SCOPE Act in TX, similar laws elsewhere) overlap with FDBR for entities serving minors.
