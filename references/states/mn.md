# Minnesota — MCDPA

**Citation form**: Minnesota Consumer Data Privacy Act, Minn. Stat. ch. 325O. Effective July 31, 2025 (with delayed effective date for postsecondary institutions through 2029). Enforced by the Minnesota Attorney General.

## Applicability

Minn. Stat. § 325O.04. Applies to persons conducting business in Minnesota or producing products/services targeted to MN residents AND during a calendar year:

1. Control/process PD of **at least 100,000** MN consumers (excluding solely-payment-transaction data); OR
2. Control/process PD of **at least 25,000** MN consumers AND derive over **25%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

Exemptions: standard.

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling with significant effects. **Right to appeal** within 45 days.

### MCDPA-distinctive rights

- **Right to obtain a list of specific third parties** (not categories) to whom the controller has disclosed PD. Same as OR. (§ 325O.05(1)(7).) Among the highest operational lifts in any state.
- **Right to question profiling decisions.** Where profiling produces a legal or similarly significant effect, the consumer has the right to:
  - Question the result of the profiling.
  - Be informed of the reason the profiling resulted in the decision.
  - Be informed of the actions the consumer may take to secure a different decision in the future.
  - Have the controller correct any inaccuracy and reevaluate the decision based on the corrected data.
  
  This is the most explicit "right to question" / "right to explanation" in any US state privacy law.

## Universal opt-out signal

Required effective Jan 1, 2026.

## Sensitive data

Standard categories. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments for high-risk activities. **Documentation requirement**: the privacy program must include reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards documented in writing.

## Minor protections

For known minors under 17, processing for targeted advertising or sale requires opt-in consent.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation**. Cure period: 30 days for entities with documented privacy programs.

## MN-specific drafting considerations

1. **Right to specific (not categorical) third parties** is a significant operational lift. Build infrastructure to log per-consumer disclosures.
2. **Right to question profiling decisions** is GDPR-Art-22-adjacent. Programs using ADM for credit, employment, housing, insurance, healthcare must be prepared to provide reasoned explanations and correction-driven re-evaluation.
3. UOOM required Jan 2026.
4. Document the written privacy program — required for cure-period eligibility.
