# Oregon — OCPA

**Citation form**: Oregon Consumer Privacy Act, Or. Rev. Stat. §§ 646A.570 to 646A.589. Effective July 1, 2024 (for non-profits, July 1, 2025). Enforced by the Oregon Attorney General.

## Applicability

ORS § 646A.572. Applies to persons conducting business in Oregon or providing products or services targeted to OR residents AND, during the preceding calendar year:

1. Controlled or processed PD of **at least 100,000** consumers (excluding solely-payment-transaction data); OR
2. Controlled or processed PD of **at least 25,000** consumers AND derived **at least 25%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

Exemptions: insurance / GLBA-aligned data narrowly; HIPAA PHI; FCRA; DPPA; FERPA; certain state agencies and tribes. **Non-profits initially in scope (July 2025 effective).**

## Consumer rights

ORS § 646A.574. Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling with significant effects. **Right to appeal** within 45 days. **List-of-third-parties right**: consumers may request the **specific third parties** to whom the controller has disclosed PD (broader than most states' "categories of third parties" right).

## Universal opt-out signal

Required effective Jan 1, 2026. Recognized signals include GPC.

## Sensitive data

ORS § 646A.570(17). Standard categories plus **status as a victim of crime** and **transgender or non-binary status** (Oregon-specific). **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

ORS § 646A.578. Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments for high-risk processing.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation**. **Cure period sunset Jan 1, 2026.**

## OR-specific drafting considerations

1. Lower 25% revenue threshold (lower than VA's 50%).
2. The right to a list of *specific* third parties is a significant operational lift — most controllers maintain only categorical disclosure. Build infrastructure to identify per-consumer third-party recipients.
3. Sensitive-data definition is broader than most states (crime victim status, transgender/non-binary status).
4. Non-profits in scope effective July 2025.
