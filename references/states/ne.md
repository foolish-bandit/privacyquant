# Nebraska — NDPA

**Citation form**: Nebraska Data Privacy Act, Neb. Rev. Stat. §§ 87-1101 to -1116. Effective Jan 1, 2025. Enforced by the Nebraska Attorney General.

## Applicability

Modeled on Texas. Persons conducting business in NE or producing products/services consumed by NE residents AND processing or engaging in the sale of PD AND **NOT a small business as defined by the SBA** — UNLESS selling sensitive PD without consent (in which case applicability extends to small businesses for purposes of consent).

> **No numerical consumer threshold** — applicability is based on SBA size status by NAICS code.

Exemptions: standard.

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling. **Right to appeal** within 60 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

**Not required.**

## Sensitive data

Standard categories. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation**. **30-day cure period** at enactment.

## NE-specific drafting considerations

1. NDPA closely tracks TDPSA (Texas) — the SBA size-based threshold and small-business sensitive-data carve-out are nearly identical.
2. UOOM not required.
3. A multi-state program built for TX largely satisfies NE.
