# Tennessee — TIPA

**Citation form**: Tennessee Information Protection Act, Tenn. Code §§ 47-18-3201 to -3215. Effective July 1, 2025. Enforced by the Tennessee Attorney General.

## Applicability

Tenn. Code § 47-18-3202. Applies to persons conducting business in Tennessee producing products/services targeted to TN residents AND exceeding **$25 million revenue** AND:

1. Control/process PD of **at least 175,000** TN consumers in a calendar year; OR
2. Control/process PD of **at least 25,000** TN consumers AND derive over **50%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

> **Highest "primary tier" threshold (175k)** of any state. Combined with the $25M revenue floor, TIPA's applicability is narrower than most states.

Exemptions: standard (GLBA, HIPAA, non-profits, higher ed, employment, B2B).

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling with significant effects. **Right to appeal** within 60 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

**Not required.**

## Sensitive data

Standard categories. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments.

## Affirmative defense — TIPA's distinctive feature

Tenn. Code § 47-18-3213. **TIPA provides an affirmative defense to enforcement** for entities that maintain a written privacy program that "reasonably conforms" to the **NIST Privacy Framework** or other documented policies, standards, and procedures designed to safeguard consumer privacy that, at the time of conduct giving rise to enforcement, is reasonably designed to:
- Protect consumer privacy.
- Be of an appropriate scale and scope considering the size and complexity of the controller, the nature and scope of activities, the sensitivity of PD processed, the cost and availability of tools, and compliance with comparable state or federal laws.

This is the **most explicit safe-harbor language in any US state privacy statute.** Documenting NIST PF alignment is high-leverage.

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation** plus possible treble damages for willful violations. **60-day cure period** in effect.

## TN-specific drafting considerations

1. **Highest applicability threshold** (175k consumers + $25M revenue) makes TIPA inapplicable to many SMBs in scope elsewhere.
2. **NIST Privacy Framework affirmative defense** is the most defensibility-positive provision in any state law. A multi-state privacy program drafted with NIST PF alignment in mind earns the affirmative defense at no marginal cost.
3. No UOOM requirement — a TN-only program does not need GPC handling.
4. Treble damages for willful violations elevate enforcement risk relative to other states despite the lower base penalty.
