# Delaware — DPDPA

**Citation form**: Delaware Personal Data Privacy Act, Del. Code tit. 6, ch. 12D, §§ 12D-101 to 12D-112. Effective Jan 1, 2025. Enforced by the Delaware Department of Justice (Consumer Protection Unit).

## Applicability

Del. Code § 12D-103. Applies to persons conducting business in Delaware or producing products/services targeted to DE residents AND during the preceding calendar year:

1. Controlled/processed PD of **at least 35,000** DE consumers (excluding solely-payment-transaction data); OR
2. Controlled/processed PD of **at least 10,000** DE consumers AND derived over **20%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

> **Tied for lowest thresholds in any state** (with NH and NJ for 10k tier; with NH for 35k tier). DPDPA reaches significantly more businesses than most state laws.

Exemptions: government; financial institutions (data-level GLBA); HIPAA (data-level); FCRA (data-level); non-profits dedicated to insurance crime / national securities associations; employment data; B2B. **Note: most non-profits are NOT exempt at the entity level — only the narrowly-defined categories listed are.**

## Consumer rights

Access, correct, delete, portability, opt-out of sale / targeted advertising / profiling with significant effects. **Right to obtain a list of categories of third parties** to whom the controller has disclosed PD. **Right to appeal** within 60 days.

## Universal opt-out signal

Required effective Jan 1, 2026.

## Sensitive data

Standard categories. **Opt-in consent required.**

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; data minimization; purpose limitation; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts; data protection assessments for high-risk activities.

## Enforcement

DOJ Consumer Protection Unit. **No private right of action.** Civil penalties under Delaware Consumer Fraud Act framework. Cure period: 60 days, but verify against current guidance.

## DE-specific drafting considerations

1. **Lowest thresholds** (10k/35k consumers) make DPDPA reach many SMBs that escape coverage in other states. Run the test independently.
2. **Non-profits are largely covered** — unlike VA/CT/CO. Non-profits operating in DE should not assume exemption.
3. UOOM mandatory effective Jan 2026.
