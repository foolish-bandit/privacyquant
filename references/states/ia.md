# Iowa — ICDPA

**Citation form**: Iowa Consumer Data Protection Act, Iowa Code §§ 715D.1 to 715D.9. Effective Jan 1, 2025. Enforced by the Iowa Attorney General.

## Applicability

Iowa Code § 715D.2. Applies to persons conducting business in Iowa or producing products/services targeted to IA consumers AND in a calendar year:

1. Controls/processes PD of **at least 100,000** IA consumers; OR
2. Controls/processes PD of **at least 25,000** IA consumers AND derives over **50%** of gross revenue from sale of PD.

Exemptions: standard (government, GLBA, HIPAA, non-profits, higher ed, employment, B2B).

## Consumer rights — narrowest regime

Iowa Code § 715D.3. **Iowa has the narrowest rights regime among comprehensive state laws.**

| Right | Available in IA? |
|---|---|
| Access | ✓ |
| Delete | ✓ |
| Portability | ✓ |
| Correct | ✗ |
| Opt out of sale | ✓ |
| Opt out of targeted advertising | ✗ |
| Opt out of profiling | ✗ |
| Right to appeal | ✗ |

Iowa's only opt-out is from sale; targeted advertising and profiling opt-outs are not provided.

## Universal opt-out signal

**Not required.** Iowa controllers may rely on a "Do Not Sell" form mechanism.

## Sensitive data

Iowa Code § 715D.1. Standard categories.

**Opt-out structure (notice + opportunity to opt out)** — *not* opt-in. Iowa is one of two states (with Utah) using opt-out for sensitive data.

## Controller duties

Privacy notice; reasonable security; non-discrimination; processor contracts. **No data protection assessment requirement.**

## Enforcement

AG only. **No private right of action.** Civil penalty up to **$7,500 per violation**. **90-day cure period** (longest of any state).

## IA-specific drafting considerations

1. Iowa has the longest response window (90 days) and the longest cure period (90 days).
2. The narrower rights regime — no targeted-ad opt-out, no correction, no profiling opt-out, no appeal — means an Iowa-only program is materially less burdensome than other states. A multi-state program built to other states' standards far exceeds Iowa's requirements.
3. UOOM not required and sensitive-data opt-out structure means a CA-targeted CMP is more than enough.
