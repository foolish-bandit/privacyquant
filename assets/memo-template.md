## 9. Limitations and assumptions

This memorandum's conclusions depend on the following assumptions. If any are inaccurate, conclusions may not hold:

1. The revenue, consumer count, and processing activity figures provided by [client] are accurate and complete for the relevant calendar year.
2. The data categories, vendors, and channels described to counsel constitute the entirety of [client]'s PD processing relevant to US state consumer privacy laws. Undisclosed processing — including activities of subsidiaries, joint ventures, or affiliates — has not been analyzed.
3. The state statutes, regulations, and AG/agency guidance cited reflect the law in effect as of the date above. Material changes (new laws, amendments, regulatory rulemakings, court decisions) may post-date this memorandum.
4. This memorandum does not address: federal sectoral laws standalone (HIPAA, GLBA, FCRA, COPPA, FERPA — though it flags overlap); non-US laws (GDPR, UK GDPR, PIPEDA, LGPD); state biometric laws standalone (BIPA, CUBI); state AI-specific laws (Colorado AI Act, NYC LL 144); state-specific consumer-health-data laws (WA MHMDA, NV health data law); state social-media laws (CA SB 976, NY SAFE for Kids, FL HB 3, TX SCOPE Act); state wiretap statutes (Cal. CIPA); litigation under Cal. Bus. & Prof. Code § 17200 (UCL); or VPPA / TCPA / CAN-SPAM / state breach notification.
